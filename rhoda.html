Rhoda

Automating the opaque, agent-driven process of UAE company formation while maintaining the high-trust referral dynamic.


First-Principles Problem Solving

By interviewing agents on the ground in the UAE, I identified that the primary friction point wasn't just bureaucracy, but a deep informational asymmetry. In its current state, the process is a 'black box' of fragmented WhatsApp threads and manual follow-ups, leaving the user completely in the dark. My goal with Rhoda was to bridge this gap by designing a system where human expertise and machine efficiency don't just coexist, but actively reinforce each other. I focused on transforming what is typically an opaque and anxious interaction into a transparent, structured, and highly efficient product experience


High-Leverage Design Decisions

01. Curated Onboarding (The Agent-as-a-Platform)

Instead of a generic wizard, the UX starts with a pre-validated plan by an expert. This leverages the agent's judgment while using the UI to provide the transparency the manual process lacks.


02. Operational Clarity (Action vs. Status)

I split the dashboard into two clear domains: Platform Responsibilities (Status/Timelines) vs. User Responsibilities (KYC/Uploads). This eliminates the "anxiety of the unknown" and reduces support overhead by clarifying where the bottleneck lies.


03. Decoupling the Critical Path

The physical agent relationship is a feature, not a legacy artifact to design around. Ahmed appears consistently across the product: in the onboarding ("Ahmed has selected..."), in the welcome modal ("Your advisor Ahmed is available 24/7"), and as a persistent sidebar element with live status. But he's never in the critical path — users can complete all required actions independently. The agent becomes a safety net, not a bottleneck.


04. Trust-Pattern Leveraging

I adopted banking UI patterns for the mobile experience. Since company formation is a high-stakes financial activity, using established "security" mental models minimized the learning curve and maximized user confidence.
