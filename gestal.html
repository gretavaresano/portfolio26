Gestaltungslehre and Design

The project is rooted in a brutalist aesthetic: direct, functional, and stripped of unnecessary ornament. The visual language is defined by a "maniacal" focus on typographic scale and white space, ensuring a rhythmic flow that guides the user through complex archival layers.

In a nod to the fundamentals of visual design, the geometry of the typeface’s letter "G" was used as the structural catalyst for the entire grid. By treating a single glyph as a modular unit, the design bridges the strict discipline of the TU Wien with a contemporary, sharp digital execution—turning a technical obsession into a distinctive visual signature.
